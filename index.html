About

Simulating the Extreme

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Czech Technical University in Prague (CTU), working in the group of Prof. Tomáš Polcar. My research centres on large-scale molecular dynamics simulations of tungsten — the leading candidate material for plasma-facing components in future fusion reactors such as ITER and DEMO.

Using millions of CPU-hours on national supercomputers, I investigate how hypervelocity dust impacts, neutron irradiation, and helium implantation alter the microstructure of tungsten at the atomic level. My work spans crater morphology, dislocation network evolution, defect classification, and thermal effects under fusion-relevant conditions.

In 2026 I was awarded a GAČR Postdoctoral Individual Fellowship (PIF OUT) to continue this research at Imperial College London, UK, where I will extend my simulations to polycrystalline tungsten and tungsten alloys.

I actively develop and share open-source computational tools for the materials science community, and I am always open to new collaborations.

Research Focus

Core Research Areas

💥

Hypervelocity Dust Impacts

Large-scale MD simulations of tungsten dust impacting plasma-facing walls at fusion-relevant velocities. Quantifying crater morphology, ejecta characteristics, and damage as functions of impact angle, velocity, and temperature.

⚛️

Radiation Damage & Defect Evolution

Characterising point defects, dislocation loops, and helium bubble formation in irradiated tungsten. Combining MD with Object Kinetic Monte Carlo (OKMC) for microstructural evolution over experimentally relevant timescales.

🔬

Dislocation Mechanics

Developing automated tools for dislocation characterisation — classifying screw, edge, and mixed segments, counting junctions, and extracting length distributions from atomistic simulation data.

🧊

Helium–Tungsten Interactions

Studying helium bubble nucleation, platelet formation, and their morphological transitions in tungsten under plasma exposure conditions. Connecting atomistic findings to mesoscale models.

🔩

High-Entropy & Tungsten Alloys

Extending hypervelocity impact and irradiation studies to W alloys and multi-component systems. Analysing short-range order, rhenium clustering, and intermetallic phase formation.

🌊

Pellet Ablation Cloud Modelling

Emission modelling of hydrogen pellet ablation clouds for fusion fuelling validation. Developing algorithms for automated parameter extraction from fast-camera imagery.
